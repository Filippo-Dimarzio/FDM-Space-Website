Cygnus X-1 Information

Cygnus X-1: One of the first and strongest black hole candidates, discovered via X-ray emissions in 1964.
Mass: Approximately 21 times the mass of our Sun, locked in a binary system with a blue supergiant.

Located roughly 6,000 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus, Cygnus X-1 is one of the strongest sources of X-rays seen from Earth. It is part of a binary system, pulling matter from a massive blue star. This matter heats up and emits powerful radiation as it spirals into the black hole.
